Our story

We're punkQ

A tight-knit team of engineers, AI researchers, and product thinkers who got tired of AI tools that feel like autocomplete with a press agent.

Our mission

Code is craft.
AI is the apprentice.

We believe developers shouldn't have to fight their tools. The best tools disappear into your workflow — they understand what you're trying to build, not just what you're typing.

punkQ started from a simple frustration: every AI coding tool felt like it was optimising for demo impressiveness, not actual developer productivity. We set out to fix that.

Three years later, we've built the most context-aware AI coding system available — one that reads your codebase, understands your patterns, and suggests changes that actually fit.

How we work

Engineering principles we refuse to compromise on

📏

Smallest credible increment

We ship the smallest thing that proves the idea before generalising. Every week there's something real to use, not just promises on a roadmap.

🧱

Boring tech first

We pick well-known stacks and justify every exotic choice in writing. Your infra shouldn't need a PhD to operate at 3am.

↩️

Reversibility by default

We prefer choices that are easy to undo and call out one-way doors explicitly before walking through them.

🔭

Read before write

We read the existing code and conventions before proposing changes. We never rewrite what already works, and we never assume we know better without looking.

🛡️

Security defaults

User input, secrets, and external services are treated with appropriate skepticism by default. Security is in the foundation, not a feature added later.

📐

Scope discipline

We don't bundle unrelated cleanups into feature tickets. Every change has a clear scope, and we land separate concerns as separate issues.

Our values

What we stand for

🔓

Radical transparency

We publish our architecture decisions, our mistakes, and our roadmap. No vaporware, no black boxes.

⚖️

Honest AI

Our AI tells you when it's uncertain. It doesn't hallucinate confident nonsense. When it doesn't know, it says so.

🌍

Developer-first

Every product decision starts with "does this make a developer's day better?" — not "does this look good in a demo?"

🚧

Bias for action

We ship, we learn, we iterate. Perfection is the enemy of shipped. We'd rather have something real in your hands than perfect in our heads.
